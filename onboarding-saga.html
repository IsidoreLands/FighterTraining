Saga #GEN-001: Orient Yourself

You have chosen to do something. Welcome. Your first task is not to write code or design a wing. It is to complete an OODA Loop. Your objective is to understand the system and identify a single point of friction—a Nemesis—and propose a solution—a new Saga.

Phase 1: OBSERVE

Your task is to find an **Echo**—an undeniable piece of feedback about the current state of the GENESIS project. This Echo can come from any domain.

Choose one of the following observation paths:

  • Path A (The Engineer): Clone the genesis-api repository. Follow the `README.md` to set it up and run the tests. The test output is your Echo.
  • Path B (The Designer): Review the current user interface design for the vehicle editor. Use this prototype link: [Figma Prototype]. Your user experience is your Echo.
  • Path C (The Doctrine Analyst): Read the project's Architectural Doctrine: [Doctrine v2.0]. The clarity or ambiguity of the document is your Echo.

Phase 2: ORIENT

Filter your Echo through the project's objectives. Identify a **Hero** (something that works well) and a **Nemesis** (a point of friction, confusion, or inefficiency). Your analysis is the synthesis of these observations.

Phase 3: DECIDE

Based on your orientation, form a decision. Your decision must be to propose a new **Saga** that either amplifies a Virtue (a pattern that creates Heroes) or destroys a Vice (a pattern that creates Nemeses).

Phase 4: ACT

Your final action is to formalize your new Saga. Go to the main project repository's Saga Proposal Template. Copy the template, fill it out with your analysis from the Orient and Decide phases, and submit it by emailing it to architect@genesis-project.dev with the subject "Saga Proposal".

This action closes your first loop and begins the next. We will review your proposal and assign you your next Saga. Your work has begun.
